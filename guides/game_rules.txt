Game Rules Detail (In-Game Mechanics)
===

For GM operational rules (narration, agents, turn processing), see guides/gm_rules.txt

Dice Mechanics
===

[Dice Types and Usage]
- Ability checks: 1d20 + modifier vs DC
- Damage rolls: Weapon/spell specific (e.g., 1d8 + STR for longsword)
- Saving throws: 1d20 + stat modifier vs DC

[Difficulty Class (DC) Guidelines]
- DC 5: Trivial (almost anyone can do it)
- DC 10: Easy (slight challenge)
- DC 13: Moderate (average difficulty)
- DC 15: Hard (requires skill)
- DC 18: Very Hard (expert level)
- DC 20: Near Impossible (legendary feat)
- Detailed values: see data/rules.json

[Critical and Fumble]
- Critical (Natural 20): Automatic success. Double damage dice on attacks.
- Fumble (Natural 1): Automatic failure. May trigger negative side effects (weapon drop, self-injury, spell backfire — GM discretion).

[Display Formats]
- Public Mode (show_dice_result: true):
  - Show all values: 1d20 -> [15] + INT(+4) = 19 vs DC 16 -> Success!
  - Damage: 1d8 -> [6] + INT(+4) = 10 damage
  - Critical: 1d20 -> [20] / Fumble: 1d20 -> [1]
- Private Mode (show_dice_result: false):
  - Do not expose numerical values in narration — convey results through description only
  - Only criticals/fumbles are indicated separately
- Narration order: Situation description -> Dice roll display (if public) -> Continue narration with result

Tactical Cover Rules
===
- When declaring "hide/take cover" on a tile adjacent to an object, a cover bonus is applied
- Cover bonus applies only to ranged attacks (not melee attacks)
- Cover is only valid when cover object is between the attacker and defender
- Use get_cover_at(position, state) to query available cover objects
- Use apply_cover(attacker_pos, defender_pos, state) to calculate cover bonus
- Use damage_object(obj_name, damage, state) to destroy objects
- Flammable objects take extra damage from fire attacks + are destroyed
- Explosive objects (oil barrels) deal area damage to surroundings when destroyed
- Agent [Rule Judge] automatically checks cover during combat

Fall Damage Rules
===
- Falling from height: 1d6 bludgeoning damage per 3 meters fallen
- Maximum: 20d6 (60m fall cap)
- DEX saving throw may halve damage (DC = 10 + 1 per 3m fallen)
- Landing on soft surfaces (water, hay, snow) reduces damage by half
- Feather Fall or similar effects negate fall damage entirely
- Detailed values: see data/rules.json

Object Stacking Rules
===
- Entities (players, NPCs, monsters) cannot stack on the same tile
- Movement into an occupied tile is blocked — the entity must choose an adjacent tile
- Exception: Allied entities may pass through (but not end turn on) an occupied tile
- Objects (items, furniture, terrain features) can coexist on the same tile as entities
- Large creatures (2x2 or larger) occupy multiple tiles — all occupied tiles are blocked

Environmental Hazard Rules
===
Tiles may have environmental effects that trigger when an entity enters or starts their turn on them.

[Fire Tiles]
- 1d6 fire damage per turn while on the tile
- Flammable equipment/items may catch fire (DEX save DC 12 to avoid)
- Fire spreads to adjacent flammable tiles at end of round
- Extinguished by water, ice magic, or smothering actions

[Poison Gas]
- 1d4 poison damage per turn while in the area
- CON saving throw DC 13 at start of each turn to halve damage
- May inflict "Poisoned" status on failed save (disadvantage on attacks/checks)
- Dissipates after 1d4+1 rounds unless source is continuous
- Wind or air magic can clear gas immediately

[Slippery/Ice Tiles]
- DEX saving throw DC 12 when entering or moving through
- Failure: fall prone (lose remaining movement, attacks against have advantage)
- Movement cost doubled on ice tiles

[Darkness/Magical Darkness]
- Blocks normal vision — attacks have disadvantage, perception checks at -5
- Darkvision reduces penalty (treat as dim light)
- Magical darkness: even darkvision fails, only truesight or dispel works

[Difficult Terrain]
- Movement cost doubled (1 tile costs 2 tiles of movement)
- Examples: rubble, thick vegetation, shallow water, steep slopes
- Detailed values: see data/rules.json

Line of Sight Rules
===
Line of sight (LoS) determines whether a character can see or target another entity.

[Basic Principle]
- Draw a straight line from attacker center to target center
- If the line passes through a solid obstacle (wall, large object), LoS is blocked
- Partial obstruction (half-wall, narrow pillar) grants cover but does not block LoS

[Ranged Attack Requirements]
- Ranged attacks require clear LoS to the target
- Melee attacks do not require LoS (adjacent tile only)
- Spells with "target you can see" require LoS

[Elevation]
- Higher ground grants LoS advantage (can see over low obstacles)
- Lower ground may have LoS blocked by terrain features
- Elevation difference of 3m+ grants +2 bonus to ranged attacks (high ground advantage)

[Darkness and Visibility]
- Dim light: LoS intact but perception checks at disadvantage
- Darkness: LoS blocked for creatures without darkvision
- Fog/smoke: LoS blocked beyond 2 tiles (heavy) or grants concealment (light)

Carry Weight / Encumbrance (Optional)
===
Lightweight reference system. Only enforced when the GM enables encumbrance tracking.

[Weight Capacity]
- Base carry capacity: STR x 5 kg
- Encumbered (over 60% capacity): Movement speed -1 tile
- Heavily encumbered (over 80% capacity): Movement speed -2 tiles, disadvantage on DEX checks
- Over capacity (100%+): Cannot move, disadvantage on all physical checks

[Common Item Weights]
- Light weapon (dagger, wand): ~0.5 kg
- Medium weapon (longsword, staff): ~1.5 kg
- Heavy weapon (greatsword, halberd): ~3 kg
- Light armor: ~5 kg
- Medium armor: ~10 kg
- Heavy armor: ~20 kg
- Potion/scroll: ~0.2 kg
- Coins (100): ~1 kg

[Simplification]
- By default, encumbrance is not tracked unless the scenario calls for it
- GM may enable encumbrance for survival/exploration-heavy scenarios
- Detailed values: see data/rules.json
